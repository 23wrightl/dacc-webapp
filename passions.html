Design


The definition of design:

Merriam-webster defines design as "to devise for a specific function or end." My definition of design is to plan out a creative idea, that serves a purpose to someone or something. This purpose must be used to help, never hurt. When I design things, I don't think about it much. Normally, I just spill all my random thoughts onto a page. I don't have much strategy or much design knowledge to back me up. Various research has helped me piece together what I know, and I only really follow what looks pleasing to me. I am excited to learn the psychology behind it though and UX design seems like a potential career path for me!

Tiny Home


What are tiny homes?

Tiny homes are a dwelling that occupy 400 square feet or less. They come in many different forms, such as living in a shed-like structure, shipping containers, and even in school buses! Many people choose this down-sizing lifestyle to cut down on money, possessions, or just to give themselves the chance to travel. A nomadic lifestyle is a big possibility when you live in a tiny home on wheels! There are hundreds of websites, youtube videos, and tutorials for how to enter this lifestyle and build your own tiny home, and hopefully, I will be able to join it too!

  • Tiny House Talk



Art


The complexities and simplicity of art:

My love for art has always stemmed from my love for design. The desire to create has always been there and I find that creativity is my greatest strength. Art just so happened to be the side-effect of that. I love to create art and I am proud of what I have created; however, I have always had issues with my perfectionism. It causes me to nitpick every detail, and it makes every project I start, daunting. The thought that it won't live up to the idea in my head is tough to deal with. Despite this, I have still pushed myself to start and finish many pieces of art and I am very happy with how many of them have turned out!

Writing


What is writing to me?

Writing comes in many different forms. There are many many genres of writing out there, and to be a writer is a great feat. I, myself, can not claim that I am a writer, I merely enjoy writing itself. Writing is not my sole intent in life, and I have many more plans outside of writing my own novel. Maybe, if I do end up writing that book, I can call myself one, but today is not that day. That does not mean I draw any less satisfaction from letting the words in my head spill onto the pages of my documents, journals, and notes. Writing is an escape, just like reading!

Reading


The theater in my head:

Once, I sat down with my brother to talk about why he didn't enjoy reading. He told me that it was boring, and all he saw was the words on the page. Hearing this news, I was shocked. Whenever I read, I always see it. It's a movie of my own creation. If I pick up a good book, the details fall into place and I watch the book as if it were a movie. It's almost like a spell. I am entraced by the book, and the words on the page seem to dissapear. When I am interrupted by the bustling of the world, the words come back to me and my stage fades away. My books are my outlet, my escape.
